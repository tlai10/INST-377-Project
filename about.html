General Information about Speed Traps

Speed cameras in Prince George’s County generated $6.8 million in revenue in 2018, with College Park alone collecting nearly $3 million in fines. Tickets and fines are given to drivers that are speeding 12mph or above the speed limit. For UMD students who are already paying thousands in tuition, unwanted speeding tickets can create additional stress. Especially for students that are not from the area, their unfamiliarity with the location of cameras can increase the risk of being fined.

In addition, there has been increasing legislation to add more speed cameras on highways to decrease accidents. In June, a new law was passed to place speed cameras on Maryland Route 210 in southern Prince George’s County. These laws have been put in place in an effort to decrease speeds on the highway, but have also created more opportunities for drivers to generate higher fines.

Especially for students driving on and around campus, the speed limit drops drastically. On Route 1 (Baltimore Ave.), the current speed limit is 25 miles per hour, a 5 mile per hour decrease since 2014. While it has been enforced in order to ensure the safety of pedestrians and students walking in the area, it has also created a dilemma for drivers who have an increasingly difficult time navigating the roads without being ticketed.

Our project aims to increase the awareness of the locations of speed cameras in order to alleviate the stress of potential fines on UMD students. With the understanding that speed limits and cameras are put into place for the safety of both drivers and pedestrians, providing drivers with the knowledge of where speed cameras are located will allow for both a safer and more affordable environment.
